Sarah Terlaga

My first job out of school was in HR, so needless to say, Kori caught my attention when she responded to a craigslist add that I had posted for a roommate back in 2011. I had met with at least 15 different people while looking for a roommate, and as soon as I met Kori, I knew she was "the one" (sorry Mike, she was "the one" for me first!). She was not only a great roommate, but an even better friend. She was the most easy-going, thoughtful person I had lived with, and I always valued her career advice since she was a few years ahead of me in the world of HR. I remember the first date she had with Mike and I basically dragged every detail out of her (as I tend to do with most of my friends). As I watched their relationship develop, I remember thinking that there was something just so easy and natural about it - no games, no tricks - just something refreshingly real. As I got to know Mike, I realized how much his selfless, genuine personality complemented Kori's so well. There was not a doubt in my mind that they would end up together, and I don't think I've ever felt so happy about another couple.

A few years later when Kori told me she was moving out to live with Mike, I was beyond thrilled for them (ok, a part of me was pretty sad, knowing I would lose Kori as a roommate), but I knew this would be an exciting next chapter in her life. I feel so fortunate to have met the both of them, and I'm honored to be a part of their special day. I wish them all of the happiness in the world, and I don't think there are two other people that deserve it more. Love you Kori and Mike!
